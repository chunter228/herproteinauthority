Strength Training and Protein: A Woman's Guide

Strength training isn’t just for bodybuilders—it’s essential for women’s health. Lifting weights preserves bone density, boosts metabolism and shapes a toned physique. To support muscle recovery, aim for 20‑30 grams of protein after workouts and spread intake throughout the day.

Many women worry about ‘bulking up,’ but this is a myth. Building significant muscle requires intensive training and high calorie intakes. Adequate protein ensures you develop lean, defined muscles without excess bulk.

Choose protein sources that align with your preferences and values: chicken, fish, beans, tofu and shakes like Koia can all fit. Pair protein with carbohydrates post‑workout to replenish glycogen and help muscles repair.
